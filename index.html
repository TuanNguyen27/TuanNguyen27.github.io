About me


I am a junior at Swarthmore College majoring in Mathematics (with Statistics emphasis) and Computer Science. My CS interests are automation of data science and machine learning pipelines (autoML), dimensionality reduction, distributed optimization and missing data. I am also passionate about philosophy, photography and classical music.

This summer, I am doing research under Professor Steve Wang at Swarthmore College and Dr. Randal Olson at the University of Pennsylvania.

Check out my blog for musings on research projects and machine learning explorations.

Projects

Brief descriptions and detailed links for the curious

Estimating The Duration And Number Of Pulses In The Cambrian Explosion

In submission to GSA 2016

Modeled true extinction times of fossil specicies using an adaptive Beta distribution and Bayesian heuristics. Used kNN classification to yield probablistic prediction on categorical labels (in terms of number of pulses).

Multi-factor Dimensionality Reduction

Under active development to support multiclass classification and feature construction on continous features

Python implementation of the MDR feature construction algorithm and integration into scikit-learn and TPOT (an autoML tool to select pipeline using genetic programing).

Debunking the myth of 10-fold cross-validation and optimizing the number of folds based on meta features of a given dataset

In submission

Through a massive benchmarking test, we have confirmed that 10-fold cross-validation (CV) yields the best bias-variance tradeoff. 3-fold CV, on the other hand, consistently underperforms the average accuracy of each algorithm. Currently exploring basic meta-features for recommendations of the number of folds in CV.
